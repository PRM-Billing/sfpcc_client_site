User Story: Bulk Patient Demographics Upload with Auto-Match & Calendar Assignment

As an SFP operations user (scheduler / front-desk), I want to upload a CSV, Excel, or PDF of patient demographics received from a medical center, So that patient records are automatically created or updated and routed to the correct medical-center calendar for that day's NP visit — eliminating manual data entry.

Context

  • NPs visit external medical centers on a rotating schedule. Each medical center has its own calendar in the dashboard.
  • The medical center sends a patient list ahead of the visit. Ops needs the demographics in the calendar in time to verify insurance and referrals.
  • A meaningful share of these patients already exist in our system — for them the upload is effectively an insurance/referral refresh, not a net-new chart.

Acceptance Criteria

  1. User selects the target calendar (medical center + visit date) and uploads a CSV, XLSX, or PDF.
  2. System extracts demographics: first/last name, DOB, gender, address, phone, email, insurance (carrier, member ID, group), referral source, PCP.
  3. Patient matching runs against the existing database:
    • Update Strong match (name + DOB exact) → flagged as Update.
    • Needs Review Weak/ambiguous match → review queue for manual confirmation.
    • New No match → flagged as New.
  4. Pre-commit review screen shows three buckets: New / Update (with field-level diff) / Needs Review. User confirms before any writes occur.

On commit & operations (included in this scope)

  1. After confirmation, patient records are created or updated and placed on the correct medical-center calendar for the visit day, with a full audit trail.
  2. Master NP calendar — Month, week, and day views with upload status and demographics summaries per visit; day-details modal from month view.
  3. Upload log — Searchable history of bulk uploads with per-batch activity (parse, review, commit).
  4. Patient master — Searchable registry to view, add, edit, and deactivate patients.

US-25: Bulk Patient Demographics Upload Patients Scheduler AI Extraction

As an SFP operations user (scheduler / front-desk), I want to upload a CSV, Excel, or PDF of patient demographics from a medical center, So that records are auto-matched and routed to the correct NP visit calendar without manual data entry.
User selects medical center + visit date, uploads a list, reviews New / Update / Needs Review buckets (with diffs on updates), then confirms. Committed patients appear on the master calendar; upload log and patient master support day-to-day operations.
Select calendar + upload → Parse / AI extract → Auto-match → Pre-commit review → Commit → Patients + appointments updated
Context

NPs visit external medical centers on a rotating schedule; each center has its own calendar in the dashboard. Centers send patient lists ahead of the visit so ops can verify insurance and referrals before the NP arrives.

Many list patients already exist in the system — the upload is often an insurance/referral refresh, not a new chart. Strong matches (name + DOB) surface as Update with field-level diffs; ambiguous matches go to Needs Review; no match is New.

Decisions Needed from SFPCC

1. Source of patient master data — What should be the source of truth for demographics in the patient master (name, DOB, contact, insurance, PCP)? For example: medical-center bulk upload, CureMD, manual entry in Patient Master, or a defined priority when more than one source disagrees?

2. Close matches on the list — When names are similar or dates of birth do not match exactly, should staff always review those rows, or may the system auto-link when MRN / account number on the list matches an existing patient?

3. Retention of uploaded center lists — After a list is committed, how long should SFPCC keep the original CSV or PDF on file for compliance (e.g. 30 days, one year, or not retained after commit)?

4. Scanned PDF rosters — What is the largest roster SFPCC expects per upload (patient count or pages)? If a scan cannot be read reliably, should staff re-upload a spreadsheet or correct rows in the review screen?

UX Specification — Master Calendar & Navigation

Requirements below reflect the agreed UX from stakeholder prototype review (May 2026). Production build should match this behavior unless SFPCC directs a change — interactive prototype →

Sidebar navigation

  • Calendar (expandable submenu): Master calendar (default month), Month view, Week view, Day view, New upload (wizard), Upload log.
  • Patient Master — top-level registry (not under Calendar).

Month view

  • Month summary banner: NP visit count, lists uploaded, total patients on calendar, aggregate New/Upd/Rev counts, upload-due and not-started counts.
  • Sun–Sat grid; each day cell shows visit cards (center short name, patient count, New/Upd/Rev tags, status color: green = uploaded, yellow = upload due, grey = not started).
  • Click day or visit card → day details modal: date, Day view shortcut, per-visit upload status, Re-upload / Upload list, patients & demographics table (6 rows default, Show more), row click → patient detail modal.
  • Full-width calendar (no persistent right panel).

Week view

  • Seven columns; up to 5 inline patient rows per committed visit (name, DOB, phone, insurance, action chip).
  • Patient row click → patient detail modal; column header Day view → switches to day view for that date.

Day view

  • One card per patient for selected NP visit day(s); prev/next day navigation.
  • Card click → patient detail modal.

Filters (all calendar views)

  • Medical center, upload status, NP provider, visit date range, free-text search.
  • Active filter chips with one-click remove; Clear all filters; result count bar.

Upload wizard (7 steps)

  1. Welcome / guided demo entry
  2. Select medical center + NP visit date (or from master calendar)
  3. Upload CSV, XLSX, or PDF (sample file + template download)
  4. Parse / AI extraction animation
  5. Column mapping (spreadsheet) or AI-mapped fields (PDF)
  6. Pre-commit review: New / Update (diffs) / Needs Review
  7. Commit summary + success (calendar preview, link to upload log)

Session activity log visible from step 1; stored on upload record after commit.
